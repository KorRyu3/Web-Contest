What is stress

Stress is a state of tension that occurs when an external stimulus is received.

To put it very simply, stress it’s the brain’s response to stimuli.
What do you imagine when you think about stress?

Is it about relationships? Is it the noise coming from next door?

Everything we experience in our lives can be a “stressor”, a stress-causing stimulus.
Because of changing environment, not only pain and anxiety, but also happiness such as going on to higher education or getting married can be a stressor.
However, stress is not absolutely bad.
On the contrary, moderate stress will increase your concentration.
For example, when you study in the library, you can concentrate more than usual, can't you?
This is also a state that you feel moderate stress in a quiet space.
In other words, stress is inevitable in human life.

"Stress = not bad!"

Stress Mechanisms

Let me explain in detail.

First, stressors that directly affect the body's internal environment are processed by the cerebral cortex and thalamus, and this information is sent to the neocortex, where the amygdala, a part of the neocortex, evaluates whether it is beneficial or harmful to one's survival.

The results are sent to the hypothalamus. The hypothalamus integrates the autonomic nervous system (sympathetic nerves and parasympathetic nerves) and the endocrine system to maintain biological balance. The autonomic nervous system and the endocrine system are combined with the immune system to homeostasis.

However, when excessive stress is applied over a long period of time, homeostasis breaks down and disease occurs.

※There are many theories about the mechanism of stress.
