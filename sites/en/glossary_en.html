Glossary

A

Amygdala

Part of the limbic system, it plays an important role in the processing and memory of sudden emotional reactions.

C

Cerebral cortex

A thin layer that extends over the surface of the cerebrum. Responsible for perception, thought, reasoning, and memory.

E

Eyelid twitches

Symptoms such as eyelid cramps, difficulty opening eyes, and dazzling outdoors.

Endocrine system

A general term for organs that have glands that secrete hormones in the body. These include the pituitary gland, pineal gland, ovaries, and testes.

G

Gastric ulcer

A disease in which the stomach is damaged by excessive secretion of gastric acid. It is caused by stress, bacteria, etc..

H

Homeostasis

It has the property of trying to maintain a constant state in the body even when the external environment changes.

Hypothalamus

Organs that regulate the autonomic nervous system and endocrine functions.

I

Immune system

A mechanism that attacks a foreign substance when it enters the body.

P

Panic disorder

Suddenly driven by anxiety and fear that "you may die as it is", illness that causes palpitation, dizziness, limb tremor, etc..

Parasympathetic nerves

One of the autonomic nervous system. In contrast to the sympathetic nervous system, it is activated when the mind and body are in a rest and relaxation.

Phobia

Symptoms of unusual fear of a particular thing.

S

Social anxiety disorder

Disease that fear of being noticed or embarrassed by others, and strong distress in crowded places such as trains, buses, and downtown.

Stressor

Stimuli that cause stress. There are many different types of stimuli.

Sympathetic nerves

One of the autonomic nervous system. it is activated during active or tense states. (increased heart rate, blood pressure, etc. )

T

Thalamus

It plays an important role in relaying senses such as sight, hearing, and somatosensory perception (sense organs such as touch that cannot be clearly seen from the outside).

The autonomic nervous system

Organs that automatically regulates the internal organs, glands, and blood vessels.It consists of two parts, the sympathetic nervous and the parasympathetic nervous.

The neocortex

This is where the processing of information, such as sight and hearing, as well as areas related to advanced mental activities, such as memory and thought, are developed.
